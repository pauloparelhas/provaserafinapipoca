## PART 1: Animal Body Coverings

Animals have different body coverings that protect them. There are 6 main types:

### 1. Fur / Hair
Animals with fur or hair are called mammals.
Examples: Lion, Panda, Pig, Human
- Fur keeps animals warm
- Humans have hair on their bodies

### 2. Feathers
Birds have feathers. Feathers help birds fly and stay warm.
Examples: Penguin, Flamingo, Parakeet (parrot), Raven
- Even penguins — which cannot fly — have feathers
- Feathers can be many different colors

### 3. Scales
Scales are hard and overlapping. Reptiles and fish have scales.
Examples: Snake, Chameleon, Lizard, Shark
- Scales protect the animal's body
- Snakes shed (change) their scales as they grow

### 4. Smooth / Moist Skin
Some animals have smooth, wet skin — no fur, no feathers, no scales.
Examples: Frog, Salamander, Dolphin, Earthworm
- Amphibians like frogs need moist skin to breathe
- Dolphins are mammals but have smooth skin

### 5. Shell
A hard outer covering that protects the animal's soft body inside.
Examples: Turtle, Snail, Crab
- A turtle carries its shell everywhere
- A snail can hide inside its shell

### 6. Exoskeleton
A hard outside skeleton — like armor on the outside of the body.
Examples: Scorpion, Lobster, Ant, Grasshopper, Dragonfly
- Insects and some crustaceans have exoskeletons
- The exoskeleton protects them like a suit of armor

## PART 2: Life Cycles

Every living thing has a life cycle — it is born, it grows, and one day it dies.

### The Life Cycle of a Butterfly (Complete Metamorphosis — 4 stages)
**Stage 1 — Eggs:** The butterfly lays tiny eggs on leaves.
**Stage 2 — Caterpillar (Larva):** A caterpillar hatches from the egg. It eats and eats to grow big.
**Stage 3 — Pupa (Chrysalis):** The caterpillar forms a hard case called a chrysalis. Inside, something magical happens!
**Stage 4 — Butterfly:** A beautiful butterfly comes out! Now it can fly and lay more eggs.

The butterfly's life cycle goes around and around — it never stops!

### The Life Cycle of a Frog
**Stage 1 — Eggs:** Frogs lay eggs in water (called frog spawn).
**Stage 2 — Tadpole:** A tadpole hatches. It lives in water and has a tail.
**Stage 3 — Froglet:** The tadpole grows legs and loses its tail.
**Stage 4 — Frog:** A fully grown frog! It can live on land and in water.

### The Life Cycle of a Snake
**Stage 1 — Egg:** Most snakes hatch from eggs.
**Stage 2 — Young snake:** A small snake grows and sheds its skin.
**Stage 3 — Adult snake:** A full-grown snake.
**Stage 4 — Death:** Like all living things, the snake grows old and dies.

### The Life Cycle of a Dog
**Stage 1 — Puppy:** A baby dog is called a puppy.
**Stage 2 — Young dog:** It grows bigger and learns new things.
**Stage 3 — Adult dog:** A strong, grown-up dog.
**Stage 4 — Old dog:** The dog grows old. It moves more slowly.

### The Life Cycle of a Human
**Stage 1 — Baby:** Tiny and needs lots of care.
**Stage 2 — Child:** Goes to school, plays, and learns (that's YOU!).
**Stage 3 — Adult:** Grows up, works, may have a family.
**Stage 4 — Elderly:** Grows old, has many memories and stories to tell.

## PART 3: Metamorphosis

### What is Metamorphosis?
Metamorphosis is a MAGICAL, real-life process where an animal changes its body form A LOT as it grows.
When an animal goes through metamorphosis, it looks very, very different from when it was born.

**Definition:** Metamorphosis is when an animal changes its body form dramatically as it grows.

### The 4 Stages of Metamorphosis (using Butterfly as the example):
1. **Eggs** — the beginning
2. **Larva** (caterpillar) — the eating stage
3. **Pupa** (chrysalis) — the transformation stage
4. **Adult** (butterfly) — the final form

### Animals that go through Metamorphosis:
- Butterfly 🦋
- Frog 🐸
- Bee 🐝
- Ant 🐜
- Ladybug 🐞
- Mosquito
- Starfish
- Newt (a type of salamander)

### Animals that do NOT go through Metamorphosis:
- Dog 🐕 — grows up but keeps the same shape
- Snake 🐍 — grows bigger but doesn't change form dramatically
- Human 🧑 — grows up but stays human-shaped!

### The Big Difference:
- Without metamorphosis: baby looks like a small version of the adult (dog, human, snake)
- With metamorphosis: the animal changes its WHOLE body (caterpillar → butterfly!)
