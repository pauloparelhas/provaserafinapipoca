## PROMPT PARA CHAT DO NOTEBOOKLM (gerar resumo/roteiro)

```
You are an enthusiastic science teacher explaining Science to a 7-year-old child (Year 2 student).
The child is preparing for a Science exam tomorrow.

Based on the documents and images provided, create a complete video script covering ALL of these topics:

TOPIC 1 — ANIMAL BODY COVERINGS
Go through each slide of the "Animal body coverings" PDF and explain:
- What is FUR/HAIR? Which animals have it? (Lion, Human, Pig, Panda) → show the slide
- What are FEATHERS? Which animals have them? (Penguin, Flamingo, Parakeet, Raven) → show the slide
- What are SCALES? Which animals have them? (Snake, Chameleon, Lizard, Shark) → show the slide
- What is SMOOTH/MOIST SKIN? Which animals have it? (Frog, Salamander, Dolphin, Earthworm) → show the slide
- What is a SHELL? Which animals have it? (Turtle, Snail, Crab) → show the slide
- What is an EXOSKELETON? Which animals have it? (Scorpion, Lobster, Ant, Grasshopper, Dragonfly) → show the slide

TOPIC 2 — LIFE CYCLES
Go through the "Life cycle" PDF slides and explain:
- The butterfly life cycle: Eggs → Caterpillar → Pupa → Butterfly (reference the circular diagram)
- The frog life cycle: Eggs → Tadpole → Froglet → Frog
- The snake life cycle: Egg → Young snake → Adult → Death
- The dog life cycle: Puppy → Young dog → Adult → Old dog
- The human life cycle: Baby → Child → Adult → Elderly

TOPIC 3 — METAMORPHOSIS
- What is metamorphosis? (definition from the class notes image)
- Show examples: butterfly, frog, bee, ant, ladybug, mosquito, starfish, newt
- The 4 stages: Eggs, Larva, Pupa, Adult
- Difference: animals WITH vs WITHOUT metamorphosis

FORMAT RULES:
- Language: English (simple, max 12 words per sentence)
- Tone: excited, warm, like a fun TV show for kids (not classroom)
- Reference each slide/image as you talk about it ("Look at this picture!", "Can you see in this slide...")
- Include "Did you know?" moments with fun facts
- After each topic: 1 quick review question
- End with: "You are ready for your exam! You've got this!"
- Duration: aim for 8-10 minutes total
```
